About Me

Hello! My name is Diana Ruth and I'm a fourth year student at The University of Texas at Austin. I am a double major in Electrical Engineering and Computer Science set to graduate in May of 2017 and I'm absolutely loving it. I'm a member of Theta Tau, a national co-ed professional engineering fraternity, and I'm the president of the Texas Equestrian Team. In my free time, you can find me running, biking, cooking, or hanging out at the barn.

Since I'm studying both electrical engineering and computer science, I've been exposed to a large variety of concepts that has provided me with a well-rounded understanding of software engineering. I've taken classes on circuits, logic design, operating systems and more, but nothing gets me as excited as web development does. I have an intense passion for designing and implementing web sites and I would love to work with web and mobile development when I graduate. I love learning new languages and frameworks in my spare time to further develop my software development skills. Click here to view my resume.

Crococonut

I created this game during my sophomore year of college after learning about Java Applets. Back then, applets were still widely supported by all major browsers, although today that has changed. The game features a crocodile that's hungry for coconuts, and the player needs to get as many coconuts as possible without hitting any bombs. The game keeps track of the score, displays instructions, and allows the user to play again after they lose. I have chosen to display the game as a video below rather than embedding the applet due to the security constraints that modern browsers have on applets. View the source code on GitHub here.

Google App Engine Blog

http://1-dot-eng-carport-107918.appspot.com/blog.jsp

This project was completed as a project for my Software Engineering and Design Lab class. It's a blog that makes use of the Google App Engine functionality to store post data. Some of the main features are:

  • a Bootstrap free theme front-end
  • Google login functionality (only logged in users can create new posts)
  • the Objectify library to simplify the Google App Engine datastore
  • a cron job that sends a daily email digest of new posts to subscribed users

View the source code on GitHub here. And feel free to add some new posts!
